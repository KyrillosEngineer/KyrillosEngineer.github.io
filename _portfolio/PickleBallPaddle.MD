# Following Appropriate Standards 

The following pickle ball paddle board was designed according to the 2026 official pickle ball rules book (USA) [1](https://usapickleball.org/docs/rules/USAP-Official-Rulebook.pdf). The rule book is full of all regulations and standards. The dimensions for example of an approved paddle board are very clear according to the rule book. The rule book states “The combined length and width of the paddle, including any edge guard and butt cap, must not exceed 24 inches (60.96 cm). The paddle length must not exceed 17 inches (43.18 cm). There is no restriction on paddle thickness.” The following design follows these measures. According to typical paddle boards, it  takes a more rectilinear shape with a length of 273mm (10.38 in). The design features a 127mm (5 in) handle area which is typical of most boards. This keeps the design well under the 17 inch length requirement (15.74 in). The width in turn was made 178 mm or approximately 7 inches making the combined length and width under 24 inches. Likewise, the board is 11mm in thickness which also is in the range of standard as pickleball paddles can go up to 13mm. Adding to the design, as noted in the assignment guidelines, there are no prohibited materials according to the 2026 official pickle ball rules book (USA) and/or delamination, holes, cracks, rough textures, or indentations on the paddle to provide an advantage.

# Components
The pickleball paddle features two nice components here. The first is a printed TPU element in the handle to improve grip. The design here features a 15mm thick handle printed to support the player.TPU is a good material here to use as it is both flexible yet durable which is both necessary for attaching the handle and for ensuring the constant movement, friction, and sweat of a typical pickle ball game. The second distinct component is an interchangeable edge guard to protect the face of the paddle when contact is made with the ground. In terms of the typical wear and tear of a game and of a paddle hitting the ground for example this is also necessary. This component was made using the sweep mechanism as it was very simple to create a guard and follow the outer shape of the paddle board. The board has a unique design in that the guard handle and paddle are interlocked together via horizontal screws. This unique design allows the handle bar to hide the screws. Nonetheless, changing the guard is simple also as it just involves moving the TPU grip down and unscrewing the guard. 

# Design Optimization

The typical paddle board now is typically 8 ounces. With the choice of PLA to design the paddle and inner handle, this was very much possible. A lightweight paddle is considered under 7.3 oz while a heavy weight is considered 8.3 oz or more. The weight of the paddle can be the preference of the player in certain situations but typically the weight is important for considering swingforces. A head heavy paddle has a higher swing force on the ball which means more power on the ball while a head light paddle is lighter and therefore quicker to swing  [2](https://www.thedinkpickleball.com/paddle-swingweight-performance/). Similar to tennis, paddle boards need to keep weight optimal and reported for the user for the best performance. How heavy a paddle board feels is highly dependent on balance. The following paddle board takes a balanced approach with both the inner handle and paddle itself being made of PLA with the outer edge and outer handle being made of TPU. Nonetheless, with the paddle itself carrying most of the surface area being made of PLA it may be considered head light. This makes the design very move friendly enhancing the speed of a user’s swing. As mentioned, with much of the weight distributed in the longer and thicker handle, this optimizes the weight and balance of the paddle.
